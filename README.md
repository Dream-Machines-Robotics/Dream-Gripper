# dream-gripper
The Dream Gripper is a hardware project at Dream Machines.

The main scope when designing this gripper was to pick up anything from the size of an o-ring, to a coffee mug. At the same time, the gripper was intended to be easily reproduceable and reliant only on standard materials. Also, the gripper was designed with a linear rail as a platform in use, results with a pivot style setup may differ.

The gripper is composed out of three major components:

A rigid PLA-CF backbone

A soft TPU95A Insert

A rubbery grip tape

The tip of the gripper was intentionally not made from a flexible Material, but rather ridgid PLA-CF with a thin layer of grip tape. This "fingernail" design allows us to exert more force on smaller pieces, while maintaining a slim profile. 

In the mid part of the gripper, a soft TPU insert allows larger objects to be seized more firmly by increasing the contact surface. Object with sharp corners profitted from this also, as the TPU wrapps the grip tape layer around the object.

Instructions for Assembly:

Print the Backbone in PLA-CF and the TPU-Insert in TPU95A. The tolerance in the slots in the backbone should be large enough for the TPU Inserts, make sure that all the supports are completely removed from both parts. A tight fit is intended. Then, lay the assembly with the gripping side down on a strip of grip tape. Draw the outlines onto the backside of the tape, and cut the shape out. Leave enough material towards the inside of the gripper, so that you can extend the griip tape layer over the tpu by a few cm on each side. Otherwise, the grip tape may loosen over time.
